What Is a Binaural Frequency

When each ear receives a slightly different tone, the brain blends them into a single rhythmic pulse — a "phantom" frequency created by the difference between the two. This is a binaural frequency. That internal rhythm can guide brain activity into a more synchronized, receptive state through a process called entrainment.

Example

Right ear: 256 Hz
Left ear: 242 Hz
Brain perceives: 14 Hz

Your brain naturally aligns with this perceived frequency — allowing you to guide your mental state intentionally.

Frequency Ranges

Delta (0.5–4 Hz)Deep sleep, healing
Theta (4–8 Hz)Meditation, creativity
Alpha (8–12 Hz)Relaxed awareness, flow
Beta (12–30 Hz)Focus, active thinking
Gamma (30–100 Hz)Peak states, integration
