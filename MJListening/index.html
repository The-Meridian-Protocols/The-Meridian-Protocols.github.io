The evening

How it works

An hour that belongs to the record

No opener. No crowd noise. No screens. Everyone settles into a quiet room, the lights come down, and one album plays the way it was made to be heard — start to finish, on a proper system, with everyone actually listening.

Doors Open

Settle in

Low light, quiet room. Find your seat, put the phone away, and settle in. (30 minutes)

A few words

The record

M.J. introduces the album — what it is, why this one, what to listen for. (3 minutes)

Relax/Listen

Side A to Runout

The album, uninterrupted. Most run 35 to 55 minutes. It's longer than you think, and shorter than you'll want.

Afterward

Decompress

Stay a few minutes if you like. Talk about what you heard, or don't. Some records need a quiet walk home. (15 minutes)

Phones on silent and put away. Minimal talking. The record gets the room.

What's next

The Listening Lounge

These sessions are the first chapter of a permanent home — part library, part record store, part reading room. A place in Tulsa built for doing one quiet thing at a time, and doing it well.

  • The listening roomTuned so the record sounds right from every single seat. Comfortable, mismatched, chosen chairs.
  • The libraryBuilt-in shelves of books and records. When no session is on, come read. Phones silent, voices low.
  • Creation roomsSmall rentable rooms for painting, drawing, charcoal — bring your materials and creativity!
  • Meditation cubbiesSmall white rooms, soft light around the floorboards, a blanket, and the option to turn everything off.
