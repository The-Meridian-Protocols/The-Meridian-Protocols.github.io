How it works

An hour that belongs to the record

No opener. No crowd noise. No screens. Twenty-eight people settle into a quiet room, the lights come down, and one album plays the way it was made to be heard — start to finish, on a proper system, with everyone actually listening.

What's being built

The Listening Lounge

These sessions are the first chapter of a permanent home — part library, part record store, part reading room. A place in Tulsa built for doing one quiet thing at a time, and doing it well.

  • The listening roomTuned so the record sounds right from every single seat. Comfortable, mismatched, chosen chairs.
  • The libraryBuilt-in shelves of books and records. When no session is on, come read. Phones silent, voices low.
  • Creation roomsSmall rentable rooms for painting, drawing, charcoal — bring your materials, look up, and the ceiling is the night sky.
  • Meditation cubbiesSmall white rooms, soft light around the floorboards, a clean blanket, and the option to turn everything off.
